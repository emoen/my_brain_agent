## Definitions & Notation

### Key Terms and Symbols

1. **Discretization**: 
 The process of approximating a continuous domain or function using a finite or discrete subset. For example, solving a differential equation on an interval $[a, b]$ by approximating the solution at discrete points $t_0, t_1, \dots, t_{n+1}$, where $t_i = a + i h$ and $h = \frac{1}{n+1}$.

2. **Error Term**: 
 The difference between the true solution $u$ and the approximate solution $v$, denoted $e = u - v$. Error estimates quantify the accuracy of approximations.

3. **Diagonally Dominant Matrix**: 
 A matrix $A = (a_{ij})$ is diagonally dominant if:

$$
|a_{ii}| - \sum_{j \neq i} |a_{ij}| > 0 \quad \text{for all } i.
$$

4. **Convergence Rate**: 
 The rate at which the approximate solution $v_h$ converges to the true solution $u$ as the discretization parameter $h \to 0$. For example, a convergence rate of $O(h^2)$ indicates that the error decreases quadratically as $h$ decreases.

5. **Contraction Mapping**: 
 A mapping $F: X \to X$ on a metric space $X$ is a contraction if there exists a constant $\alpha \in (0, 1)$ such that:

$$
d(Fx, Fy) \leq \alpha d(x, y) \quad \forall x, y \in X.
$$

6. **Projection**: 
 A linear operator $P: X \to X$ is a projection if $P^2 = P$. In a Hilbert space, a projection is **orthogonal** if $x - Px \perp V$ for all $x \in X$, where $V = \text{Range}(P)$.

7. **Lagrange Interpolation Operator**: 
 For $x \in C[a, b]$, the operator $L$ is defined as:

$$
Lx = \sum_{i=1}^n x(t_i) \ell_i, \quad \text{where } \ell_i(s) = \prod_{\substack{j=1 \\ j \neq i}}^n \frac{s - t_j}{t_i - t_j}.
$$

Here,$t_1, t_2, \dots, t_n$ are distinct points in $[a, b]$, and $\ell_i$ are Lagrange basis polynomials.

## Theorems, Lemmas & Corollaries

### Lemma 1: Diagonal Dominance and Nonsingularity 
- **Statement**: 
 If an $n \times n$ matrix $A$ is diagonally dominant, then $A$ is nonsingular, and:

$$
\|A^{-1}\|_\infty \leq \max_{1 \leq i \leq n} \left( |a_{ii}| - \sum_{j \neq i} |a_{ij}| \right)^{-1}.
$$

### Lemma 2: Finite Difference Approximations for Derivatives

### Theorem 6: Convergence of Projection Methods 
- **Statement**: 
 Let $\{P_n\}$ be a sequence of projections on a Banach space $X$, and assume $P_n y \to y$ for each $y \in X$. Let $b \in X$ and $A \in \mathcal{L}(X, X)$. For each $n$, let $x_n$ satisfy:

$$
P_n(Ax_n - b) = 0.
$$

If $x_n \to x$, then $Ax = b$.
