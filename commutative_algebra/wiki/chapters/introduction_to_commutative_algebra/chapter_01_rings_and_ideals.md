## Definitions & Notation

### Ring
A **ring** $A$ is a set equipped with two binary operations (addition and multiplication) satisfying:
1. $A$ is an abelian group under addition:
   - $A$ has a zero element, denoted $0$.
   - Every $x \in A$ has an additive inverse $-x$.
2. Multiplication is associative: $(xy)z = x(yz)$.
3. Multiplication is distributive over addition: $x(y + z) = xy + xz$ and $(y + z)x = yx + zx$.
4. Multiplication is commutative: $xy = yx$ for all $x, y \in A$.
5. $A$ has an identity element, denoted $1$, such that $x1 = 1x = x$ for all $x \in A$.

### Ideal
An **ideal** $a$ of a ring $A$ is a subset of $A$ such that:
1. $a$ is an additive subgroup.
2. $Aa \subseteq a$ (i.e., $x \in A$ and $y \in a$ imply $xy \in a$).

### Prime Ideal
An ideal $p \subseteq A$ is **prime** if $p \neq (1)$ and $xy \in p$ implies $x \in p$ or $y \in p$.

### Maximal Ideal
An ideal $m \subseteq A$ is **maximal** if $m \neq (1)$ and there is no ideal $a$ such that $m \subset a \subset (1)$ (strict inclusions).

### Nilradical
The **nilradical** $\mathfrak{N}$ of a ring $A$ is the set of all nilpotent elements in $A$. It is an ideal, and $A/\mathfrak{N}$ has no nilpotent elements $\neq 0$.

### Jacobson Radical
The **Jacobson radical** $\mathfrak{m}$ of $A$ is the intersection of all maximal ideals of $A$. It can be characterized as:

$$
x \in \mathfrak{m} \iff 1 - xy \text{ is a unit in } A \text{ for all } y \in A.
$$

### Quotient Ring
The **quotient ring** $A/a$ is the ring formed by the cosets of $a$ in $A$. It inherits a uniquely defined multiplication from $A$.

### Zero-Divisor
A **zero-divisor** in a ring $A$ is an element $x \in A$ such that there exists $y \neq 0$ in $A$ with $xy = 0$.

### Integral Domain
An **integral domain** is a ring with no zero-divisors, $0 \neq 1$, and $A \neq 0$.

### Principal Ideal
The **principal ideal** generated by an element $x \in A$ is the set of all multiples of $x$, denoted $(x)$ or $Ax$.

### Field
A **field** is a ring $A$ in which $1 \neq 0$ and every non-zero element is a unit.
