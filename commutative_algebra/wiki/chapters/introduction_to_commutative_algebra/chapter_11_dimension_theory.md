## Definitions & Notation

1. **Dimension of a Ring ($\dim A$)**: The maximum length of a chain of prime ideals in a commutative ring $A$:

$$
\dim A = \sup \{n \mid \mathfrak{p}_0 \subsetneq \mathfrak{p}_1 \subsetneq \cdots \subsetneq \mathfrak{p}_n, \, \mathfrak{p}_i \text{ prime in } A\}.
$$

2. **Noetherian Local Ring**: A commutative ring $A$ with a unique maximal ideal $m$ such that every ideal of $A$ is finitely generated.

3. **$m$-Primary Ideal**: An ideal $q$ in a Noetherian local ring $A$ is $m$-primary if $\sqrt{q} = m$, where $m$ is the maximal ideal of $A$.

4. **System of Parameters**: A set of elements $x_1, \dots, x_d$ in a Noetherian local ring $A$ of dimension $d$ that generates an $m$-primary ideal.

5. **Hilbert Function**: For a Noetherian graded ring $A = \bigoplus_{n=0}^\infty A_n$ and a finitely generated graded $A$-module $M$, the Hilbert function is defined as $\lambda(M_n)$, where $\lambda$ is an additive function (e.g., length) on the class of finitely generated $A_0$-modules.

6. **Poincaré Series**: The generating function of the Hilbert function, defined as:

$$
P(M, t) = \sum_{n=0}^\infty \lambda(M_n)t^n \in \mathbb{Z}[[t]].
$$

7. **Regular Local Ring**: A Noetherian local ring $A$ of dimension $d$ is regular if it satisfies any of the following equivalent conditions:
   - $G_m(A) \cong k[t_1, \dots, t_d]$, where $k = A/m$ and $t_1, \dots, t_d$ are independent indeterminates.
   - $\dim_k (m / m^2) = d$.
   - The maximal ideal $m$ can be generated by $d$ elements.

8. **Graded Ring Associated to an Ideal**: For a ring $A$ and an ideal $a$, the graded ring $G_a(A)$ is defined as:

$$
G_a(A) = \bigoplus_{n \geq 0} a^n / a^{n+1}.
$$

9. **Characteristic Polynomial**: For a Noetherian local ring $A$ and an $m$-primary ideal $q$, the characteristic polynomial $X_q(n)$ is defined as $X_q(n) = \lambda(A/q^n)$ for sufficiently large $n$.

10. **Transcendence Degree**: For a finitely generated extension $K$ of a field $k$, the transcendence degree of $K$ over $k$ is the maximum number of algebraically independent elements in $K$ over $k$.

## Theorems, Lemmas & Corollaries

### Theorem 11.1 (Hilbert, Serre)
**Statement**: The Poincaré series $P(M, t)$ of a finitely generated graded $A$-module $M$ is a rational function of the form:

$$
P(M, t) = \frac{f(t)}{\prod_{i=1}^s (1 - t^{k_i})},
$$

where $f(t) \in \mathbb{Z}[t]$.

---

### Corollary 11.2
**Statement**: If each $k_i = 1$, then for all sufficiently large $n$, $\lambda(M_n)$ is a polynomial in $n$ (with rational coefficients) of degree $d - 1$, where $d$ is the order of the pole of $P(M, t)$ at $t = 1$.

---

### Proposition 11.3
**Statement**: If $x \in A_k$ is not a zero-divisor in $M$, then $d(M/xM) = d(M) - 1$.

---

### Proposition 11.4
**Statement**: Let $A$ be a Noetherian local ring, $m$ its maximal ideal, $q$ an $m$-primary ideal, $M$ a finitely generated $A$-module, and $(M_n)$ a stable $q$-filtration of $M$. Then:
1. $M/M_n$ is of finite length for each $n \geq 0$.
2. For all sufficiently large $n$, the length $\lambda(M/M_n)$ is a polynomial $g(n)$ of degree $\leq s$, where $s$ is the least number of generators of $q$.
3. The degree and leading coefficient of $g(n)$ depend only on $M$ and $q$, not on the filtration chosen.

---

### Corollary 11.5
**Statement**: For all large $n$, the length $\lambda(A/q^n)$ is a polynomial $X_q(n)$ of degree $\leq s$, where $s$ is the least number of generators of $q$.

---

### Theorem 11.14 (Dimension Theorem)
**Statement**: For any Noetherian local ring $A$, the following three integers are equal:
1. The maximum length of chains of prime ideals in $A$.
2. The degree of the characteristic polynomial $X_q(n)$ for any $m$-primary ideal $q$.
3. The least number of generators of an $m$-primary ideal of $A$.

---

### Corollary 11.17 (Krull's Principal Ideal Theorem)
**Statement**: Let $A$ be a Noetherian ring, and let $x \in A$ be an element that is neither a zero-divisor nor a unit. Then every minimal prime ideal $\mathfrak{p}$ of $(x)$ has $\text{height}(\mathfrak{p}) = 1$.
